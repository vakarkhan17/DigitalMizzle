Course overview

Follow a disciplined, authorized assessment workflow.

This practical path connects enumeration, vulnerability understanding, privilege escalation awareness, and reporting inside learner-owned VMs, deliberately vulnerable machines, and written-permission environments.

It teaches methodology and evidence habits without stealth, evasion, malware, persistence, credential attacks, or instructions aimed at public and third-party systems.

Learning objectives

What you will learn

Four connected phases that reflect a professional, authorized security assessment lifecycle.

01

Enumerate methodically

Validate scope, identify lab services, and build an evidence-based target profile.

02

Understand vulnerabilities

Read advisories, map evidence to lab conditions, and validate safely without public targets.

03

Analyze privilege boundaries

Recognize permissions and misconfiguration patterns from a defensive lab perspective.

04

Report professionally

Convert technical observations into risk, impact, remediation, and executive communication.

05

Preserve evidence

Capture commands, timestamps, results, screenshots, and limitations consistently.

06

Troubleshoot safely

Validate addresses, routes, service states, and assumptions before changing methodology.

07

Respect boundaries

Stop when scope is unclear and never extend a lab exercise to real systems.

08

Clean up responsibly

Remove temporary lab artifacts, restore snapshots, and document the final state.
